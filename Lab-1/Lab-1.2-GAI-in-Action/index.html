Lab 1.2 - GAI in Action

In this lab, youll explore how generative AI tools like ChatGPT and DALLE can support and enhance library communication, creativity, and engagement. From generating book recommendations and analyzing documents to designing flyers and newsletters, youll get hands-on experience crafting effective prompts and creating content. This lab will help you see the real-world potential of AI in library settingswhether it's promoting events, supporting readers, or simplifying research tasks. Get ready to experiment, create, and discover what AI can do for your library!

Learning Objectives

  1. Write effective ChatGPT prompts to generate diverse library-related content such as newsletters, flyers, blog posts, and quiz questions.
  2. Create AI-generated images to complement written materials for library promotions and communications.
  3. Understand how generative AI can enhance library outreach, communication, and engagement with students and patrons.

Exercise 1: Book Recommendations

In this hands-on exercise, you'll explore how to use ChatGPT to generate personalized book recommendations for different age groups, genres, and interests by crafting effective prompts. This activity will help you understand the power of prompting and how AI can assist in supporting readers and library users.

Step 1: Open ChatGPT in a New Tab and Sign In

ChatGPT

Step 2: Give ChatGPT some Background Info

Click on your profile in the top right of the screen. Then click "Customize ChatGPT." This allows you to enter information into the GPT about yourself for it to give you the best output. Additionally you can customize how you want ChatGPT to respond (professionally, conversationally, like a gen alpha, etc.)

For this activity, make up a character and tell the GPT their age, favorite genres, books they have read and liked (few-shot prompting!), and books they did not like in the "Anything else ChatGPT to know about you?" section.

Step 3: Test it out!

Now in a New Chat, ask it for some book recommendations! Because you already specified your preferences in the "Customize ChatGPT" screen, you should not have to specify again what this character's favorite books are.

Play around with different book preferences and see what ChatGPT recommends!

Examples:

Teenager who likes mystery novels
Graphic novels about coming-of-age stories
Children's books with diverse characters

After ChatGPT has analyzed the document, try this prompt:

Exercise 3: Creating Library Flyers and Newsletters Using Generative AI

In this activity, you will learn how to use ChatGPT and generative tools to create promotional content such as flyers and newsletters for the library. Youll practice writing prompts to generate event flyers, weekly newsletters, and corresponding images using AI.

Step 1: Start a New Chat

Create a new chat by clicking the button in the top left.

Part A: Writing a Weekly Library Newsletter

Step 2: Use a Prompt to Create a Newsletter

Copy the prompt into the new chat:

You are a librarian at a community college. Write a friendly, engaging weekly newsletter for students. Include:

  • A new book or featured collection
  • An upcoming event or workshop
  • A study tip of the week
  • Library hours for the weekend
  • Keep it under 200 words and use a casual tone.

Step 3: Review and Customize

Ask ChatGPT to rewrite or adjust the tone, layout, or content if needed.

Optional: Ask it to format the newsletter for email or print.

Once you're happy with your edited newsletter, copy your result into this Padlet!

Part B: Creating a Flyer for a Library Event

Step 4: Prompt ChatGPT to Write a Flyer Text

Copy this prompt:

Create a one-page flyer text for an upcoming Lakeside Library event:
"Poetry Night Open Mic Thursday at 6 PM in the Reading Lounge. Free snacks provided. Bring your own poem or read a favorite." Make it eye-catching, informative, and fun.

Step 5: Ask for alternative versions

You can try variations for different audiences:

Can you rewrite the flyer for social media use?
Can you add a short quote about poetry to make it more inspiring?

Once you're happy with your flyer text, copy your result into this Padlet!

Part C: Generating Images to Match the Flyer or Newsletter

Step 6: Generate a Visual Using DALL-E (available in ChatGPT)

Use this prompt:

Create an image of a cozy college library reading lounge with students performing poetry in the evening. Include warm lighting and bookshelves.

Step 7: Review and Download the Image

After generation, click to enlarge and download the image if you like it. You can also ask ChatGPT to try a different style (e.g., watercolor, digital art, realistic).

Once you're happy with your image, copy your result into this Padlet!

Part D: Writing a Library Blog Post

Step 8: Prompt ChatGPT to Write a Blog Post

Copy this prompt:

Write a 300-word blog post for the library blog introducing new graphic novels added this month. Mention at least three titles and describe why students might enjoy them. Write in an engaging, informative tone.

Step 9: Adjust for Tone, Length, or Audience

You can ask:

Can you rewrite this post for younger readers?

or

Add a call-to-action encouraging students to visit the library and check out the books.

Once you're happy with your blog, copy your result into this Padlet!

Reflection Questions

  1. What types of content did AI help you create most effectively?
  2. How can blog posts support student engagement with library resources?
  3. What other communications could AI help the library team generate?
